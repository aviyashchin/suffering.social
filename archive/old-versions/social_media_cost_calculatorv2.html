‚ö†Ô∏è Important Caveats & Research Context
  • Attribution evidence: Based on quasi-experimental studies (Braghieri et al. 2022) and systematic reviews
  • Population estimates: Derived from CDC WONDER database and peer-reviewed studies (Twenge et al. 2018)
  • QALY calculations: Follow WHO Global Burden of Disease methodology
  • Economic costs: Based on Greenberg et al. (2021) healthcare cost analysis
  • Economic burden baseline: Built on validated $326B burden (2020) with 61% workplace, 4% suicide-related costs
  • Disability impact: Documented 400M disability days/year in US for ages 15-44 via NHIS surveys
  • Dose-response relationship: PNAS 2019 shows each hour of social media ‚Üí 0.23-point increase in depressive symptoms
  • Incremental societal burden: $333.7B total including mortality ($9.6B) and disability ($4.6B) costs
  • Additional research: JID 2017, AJPM 2024, AER 2019, Hogrefe 2019, SSRN 2022, Cyberpsychology 2021
Research context: Findings draw on collaborative reviews from multiple research institutions and meta-analyses. See tooltips for detailed citations.
